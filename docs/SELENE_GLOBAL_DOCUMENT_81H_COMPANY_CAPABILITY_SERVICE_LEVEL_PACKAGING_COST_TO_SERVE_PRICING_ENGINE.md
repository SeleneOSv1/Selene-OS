## 1. Purpose

Document 81H is Selene’s **company capability, service-level, packaging, and cost-to-serve pricing engine**.

It answers:

```text
What can this company actually provide that changes customer value, price, margin, offer, service package, brand support, or B2B eligibility?
```

81H does **not** decide final price.

```text
81H = company capability + service cost/value signal.
81 Core = final price / offer / approval / audit.
```

Simple version:

```text
81H tells Selene what the company can actually do, what it costs, what value it adds, and whether it supports the price or offer.
```

A company that sells a handbag in a plastic bag is not the same as a company that sells a handbag with luxury wrapping, same-day delivery, personal styling, and flawless returns.

One sells a product.

The other sells an experience.

Pricing needs to know the difference, because apparently boxes, humans, time slots, vans, ribbons, installers, and customer patience all cost money.

---

## 2. Core 81H Law

```text
Selene must not treat product price as complete unless it has considered the company’s real capability to serve, package, deliver, support, install, customize, recover, and fulfill the customer promise as complete unless it has considered the company’s real capability.

Every service-level or capability-based price decision must consider:
- capability availability
- capability cost
- capability value
- capacity
- staff skill
- service reliability
- location coverage
- time availability
- SLA truth
- required human tasks
- brand alignment
- B2B service suitability
- customer segment fit
- risk and failure cost
- audit evidence
```

81H must prevent:

```text
charging premium without premium service
promising same-day delivery without capacity
offering luxury packaging without packaging capability
approving B2B channels that cannot support the product
including installation without qualified staff
offering service packages with negative margin
using service offers that overload operations
selling “white-glove” service with cardboard-box execution
```

No fake service promises.

No brochure fantasy.

No “we offer VIP service” if the only VIP is Dave with tape.
