## 1. Purpose

Document 81A is Selene’s **Market Pricing Intelligence + Competitive Research Engine**.

It does **not** decide the final selling price.

It studies the market and gives Document 81 clean, normalized, comparable, fresh, confidence-scored market evidence.

Simple rule:

```text
81A studies the market.
81 decides the price.
```

81A answers:

```text
What are comparable products/services selling for?
Are those comparisons fair?
Are they same quantity?
Same quality?
Same brand tier?
Same package?
Same service level?
Same delivery terms?
Same trust level?
Same market?
Same season/version/model?
Same availability?
Same all-in customer cost?
```

It prevents Selene from being fooled by:

```text
fake competitor prices
clearance dumps
wrong pack sizes
old models
last-season stock
used/refurbished products
unauthorized sellers
grey-market sellers
bad-service vendors
hidden delivery fees
headline prices with checkout surprises
different warranty/return terms
stale market data
```

A competitor price is not useful just because it exists. A mystery $9 listing from a suspicious seller does not define the market. It defines the internet having a day.

---

## 2. Core 81A Law

```text
Selene must not treat a competitor price as comparable until it has checked market context, quantity, quality, package inclusions, service level, vendor trust, availability, delivery, tax/fees, season/model/version, and data confidence.

81A must produce market intelligence, not final pricing decisions.

81A must report uncertainty honestly.
```

Every market comparison must answer:

```text
Is this really comparable?
Is this price current?
Is the item available?
Is the seller trusted?
Is the price all-in?
Is this a clearance or promotional distortion?
Is this the same quantity and quality?
Is the service/package the same?
Can Selene prove the source?
```

No “competitor is cheaper” nonsense without context. That is browser-tab pricing, and we are trying to be less embarrassing.
