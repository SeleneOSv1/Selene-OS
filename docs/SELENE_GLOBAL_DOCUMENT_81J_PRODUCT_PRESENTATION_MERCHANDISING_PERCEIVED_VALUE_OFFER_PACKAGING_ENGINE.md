## 1. Purpose

Document 81J is Selene’s **product presentation, merchandising, perceived value, and offer packaging engine**.

It answers:

```text
Does the way this product or service is shown make the customer believe the price is fair, premium, cheap, trustworthy, exciting, urgent, luxurious, official, safe, or valuable?
```

81J does **not** decide final price.

```text
81J = presentation / perceived value / merchandising signal.
81 Core = final price / offer / approval / audit.
```

Simple version:

```text
81J tells Selene whether the product looks, reads, explains, proves, and presents itself well enough to support the price.
```

Same product. Different photo. Different story. Different packaging. Different trust. Different perceived value.

Humans, tragically, have eyes.

---

## 2. Core 81J Law

```text
Selene must not treat a product or service as price-ready unless the presentation supports the intended price, brand, offer, channel, and customer expectation.

Every presentation-sensitive pricing decision must consider:
- media quality
- content completeness
- product truth
- variant accuracy
- offer clarity
- trust signals
- brand compliance
- claim evidence
- accessibility
- localization
- price transparency
- return/warranty clarity
- service promise accuracy
- customer segment fit
- channel display rules
- B2B/official-channel display restrictions

- audit evidence
```

81J must prevent:

